## 2. Origin Story

### The Gap No One Filled
India has 10Cr+ demat accounts, 44,000+ AMFI distributors, and 50,000+ SEBI-registered intermediaries — yet the ecosystem lacked a unified, trust-verified platform for professionals to connect.

Three systemic problems persisted:
- **The Trust Gap**: Retail investors couldn't distinguish genuine advisors from unregistered operators. Misinformation spread unchecked on generic social platforms.
- **Fragmented Networks**: Financial professionals operated in silos — informal chat groups, closed forums, paid newsletters. No unified, verified platform existed.
- **Knowledge Void**: Quality market insights were scattered. Generic platforms didn't verify SEBI numbers or AMFI ARNs. Nobody verified credentials.

### Evolution Timeline
| Year | Milestone | Significance |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| 1992 | SEBI Established | India's capital markets regulator formalises the financial ecosystem |
| 2009 | AMFI Grows | Mutual fund distribution professionalises with ARN registration — but networking stays offline |
| 2015 | Digital India Push | UPI, Jan Dhan, and digital KYC accelerate financial inclusion — but professional networks don't evolve |
| 2020 | Retail Explosion | 10Cr+ demat accounts. WhatsApp groups become the de-facto "network" — unverified, unregulated |
| 2024 | findoo is Born | The first financial network combining identity verification, regulatory compliance, and professional networking |

### Brand Lineage
findoo consolidates the scope of two previously separate initiatives:
- **Finterest** — early concept for financial interest-based discovery
- **Findora** — professional directory and networking prototype

Both were unified into findoo as a single, comprehensive financial network ecosystem.

- Include a blurred gradient blob (blur-3xl) for depth

## 5. Role-Based Architecture

findoo enforces strict role-based access control aligned with regulatory status:

- **Issuers** (AMCs, NBFCs, Insurance Companies, Banks): Can list and showcase regulated financial products. Cannot provide individual advice.
- **Intermediaries** (MFDs, RIAs, Insurance Agents, CAs, CFAs): Can publish Opinions classified as Education or Sentiment Signal. Cannot issue buy/sell recommendations.
- **Enablers** (KRAs, Depositories, RTAs, Custodians, Vault Managers, ASBA Banks, ESG Rating Providers): India's Market Infrastructure Institutions (MIIs) and RegTech/FinTech providers. Provide API access, infrastructure services, and B2B discovery. Cannot provide individual financial advice.
- **Investors** (Individual and Institutional): Can post Requirements (pull-based intent signals). Cannot publish financial opinions or tips.

This role-based separation is regulatory architecture — not a product feature.

## 12. UX Principles

### Trust-First Design
Every design decision prioritises trust signals:
- Verification badges are visible on every profile, post, and card
- Credential information (ARN, SEBI Reg, IRDAI License) is prominently displayed — never hidden behind clicks
- Role-based colour coding (Royal Blue for Issuers, Deep Emerald for Intermediaries, Amber Gold for Investors, Cyan Teal for Enablers) provides instant visual classification
- Trust proximity indicators (circle tiers) in the feed show connection strength

### Verification-Forward Flows
- Onboarding gates professional features behind credential verification
- Unverified profiles receive clear, non-punitive prompts to complete verification
- Verification status is a first-class data attribute — not an afterthought badge

### Role-Gated Experience
- UI adapts based on user role — Issuers see Showcase management, Intermediaries see Opinion publishing, Enablers see API/infrastructure tools, Investors see Requirements composer
- Navigation, permissions, and content visibility are all role-aware
- Role switching is explicit and auditable — never implicit

### Progressive Disclosure
- Complex features (Vault, Analytics, Gamification) reveal depth gradually
- Empty states use branded illustrations with clear next-action prompts
- Settings and advanced controls are accessible but never overwhelming on first encounter

### Inclusive Defaults
- Both light and dark themes maintain AA contrast standards
- All interactive elements are keyboard-navigable with visible focus indicators
- Animations respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Form inputs have associated labels and programmatically-linked error messages

## 13. Accessibility Standards

### Compliance Level
findoo targets **WCAG 2.1 Level AA** conformance, aligned with:
- Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 (RPwD Act) — Section 42
- Guidelines for Indian Government Websites (GIGW) — voluntary alignment
- UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (UNCRPD)

### Implemented Features
- Semantic HTML (proper headings H1–H6, landmarks, ARIA attributes)
- Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements (Tab, Enter, Escape, Arrow keys)
- Skip-to-main-content link for keyboard users
- Focus management with visible focus indicators and proper focus trapping in modals
- Minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio (AA standard) across both themes
- Browser text zoom support up to 200% without content loss
- Alt text on all informational images
- `prefers-reduced-motion` support for all framer-motion animations
- Screen reader compatibility: NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver (macOS/iOS), TalkBack (Android)

### Known Limitations
- User-generated content (uploaded images) may lack alt text
- Third-party embeds may not fully meet accessibility standards
- Complex data visualisations provide data tables as alternatives but may not be fully navigable
- Real-time features may have limited screen reader support in certain configurations

### Feedback Channel
Accessibility issues: compliance@findoo.in (Subject: "Accessibility Feedback")
Response SLA: 5 business days; critical barriers addressed within 30 days

## 19. Neutrality & Data Provenance

- **Non-Intermediary Status**: findoo does not earn commissions on products or provide individual investment advice
- **Verified-at-Source Data**: 100% of professional users are verified at onboarding against public regulatory registries
- **Signal Attribution**: Opinions are aggregated sentiment signals; individual professionals may remain publicly anonymous, but credentials are cryptographically linked for regulatory auditability
- **Investor Intent Restriction**: Investor feed activity is limited to "Requirements" (pull-based marketing) to prevent unverified tips and market manipulation
